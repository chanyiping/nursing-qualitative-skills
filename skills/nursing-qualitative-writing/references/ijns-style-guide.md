# IJNS-style guide for nursing qualitative writing

## Core logic

IJNS-style qualitative writing usually emphasises:

1. Internationally relevant nursing or health-care problem.
2. Specific population, context, culture, policy environment, or care situation.
3. Literature synthesis that advances an argument.
4. A precise qualitative knowledge gap.
5. Methodological transparency and coherence.
6. Findings written as analytic interpretations.
7. Discussion that moves from themes to conceptual contribution.
8. Concrete implications for nursing knowledge, practice, education, management, or policy.

## Avoid

- Generic openings.
- Unsupported claims of novelty.
- Theme listing without analytic meaning.
- Quotations without interpretation.
- Results repeated as discussion.
- Overgeneralised or causal claims.
