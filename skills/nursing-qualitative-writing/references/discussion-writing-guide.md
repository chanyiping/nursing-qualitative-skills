# Discussion writing guide

## Core structure

1. Main findings.
2. Literature dialogue.
3. Conceptual contribution.
4. Implications.
5. Strengths and limitations.
6. Future research.
7. Conclusion.

## Strong discussion

A strong Discussion does not simply repeat themes. It identifies the central analytic contribution of the study.

Useful analytic moves include:

- transforming descriptive themes into explanatory concepts;
- identifying tensions or transitions;
- explaining relational, organisational, cultural, or policy contexts;
- comparing findings with prior literature;
- linking findings to nursing implications;
- distinguishing empirical findings, interpretation, and recommendations.
