# Results writing guide

## Core structure

1. Participant characteristics.
2. Overview of themes, categories, trajectories, or personas.
3. Theme-level analytic paragraphs.
4. Subtheme explanations.
5. Participant quotations.
6. Integrative summaries.

## Strong qualitative results

Strong Results sections present findings as analytic interpretations, not a catalogue of quotes.

Each theme should:

- define the theme;
- explain its scope;
- clarify its relation to the research question;
- include selected quotations;
- interpret the meaning of those quotations;
- show relationships across subthemes where relevant.

## Persona-based results

Each persona should include:

- name;
- included participants;
- core description;
- characteristics;
- needs or support preferences;
- quotations;
- distinction from other personas;
- implications for tailored intervention.
