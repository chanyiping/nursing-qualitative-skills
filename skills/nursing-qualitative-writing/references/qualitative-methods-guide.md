# Qualitative methods guide

## Strong methods sections should report

- qualitative design and methodological orientation;
- target population and setting;
- sampling and recruitment;
- inclusion and exclusion criteria;
- data collection procedures;
- interview or focus group details;
- researcher characteristics and reflexivity;
- data analysis process;
- rigour and trustworthiness;
- ethical considerations.

## Methodological congruence

Do not mix procedures from incompatible qualitative traditions without justification.

For example:

- Reflexive thematic analysis should not be written as if inter-coder reliability is the main quality criterion.
- Qualitative content analysis may include coding discussion and category refinement.
- Phenomenology should attend to lived experience, meaning units, and phenomenological reduction where appropriate.
- Persona-based analysis should explain how data are transformed into persona dimensions and profiles.
